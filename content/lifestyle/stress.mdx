## 1. Overview

Chronic psychological stress activates the HPA axis, elevates cortisol, accelerates telomere attrition, and drives **inflammaging** — often negating supplement benefits.

:::evidence tier="A"
Meditation and breathwork interventions show measurable telomerase activity increases and hs-CRP reductions in randomized trials. HRV biofeedback improves autonomic balance within 4–8 weeks.
:::

:::visual
**Suggested visual:** HPA axis cascade — stressor → cortisol curve (acute vs chronic) → downstream hallmark damage map (telomeres, inflammation, sleep, genomic instability).
:::

## 4. HRV training guide

| Metric | Baseline | 8-week target | Tool |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| rMSSD | Record 7-day avg | ↑ 10–20% | Oura, Whoop, HRV4Training |
| Resting HR | Record | ↓ 2–5 bpm | Wearable |
| Stress score | Record | ↓ trend | App-dependent |

**Morning readiness:** Low HRV + poor sleep = reduce training intensity; do not add stack compounds to compensate.

## 5. Stress-compound interactions

| Compound | Note |
| --- | --- |
| NAC (GlyNAC) | May blunt some oxidative signaling needed for exercise adaptation — timing matters |
| High-dose antioxidants | Under chronic stress + overtraining, may mask adaptive signals |
| Resveratrol | No direct stress interaction; PM timing supports sleep hygiene |

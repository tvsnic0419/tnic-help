> **TL;DR** — Nutrition modulates mTOR/AMPK, supplies methyl donors, and feeds the microbiome. Non-negotiables: 8–10h eating window, 30–40g fiber, 30+ plant species/week, 1.2–1.6 g/kg protein. Track fiber grams and eating window daily — food outranks pills for nutrient sensing and dysbiosis.

## 1. Why this protocol comes first

Nutrition is the **daily nutrient sensing input** — every meal is an mTOR/insulin signal. It supplies methyl donors for epigenetic maintenance, glycine/cysteine for endogenous GSH, and prebiotic substrate for microbiome diversity.

Time-restricted eating, adequate protein, and polyphenol-rich diets show measurable effects on epigenetic clocks, insulin sensitivity, and inflammatory markers in human trials — at zero marginal cost beyond groceries.

**Hallmarks primarily affected:** Disabled macroautophagy · Disabled autophagy · Epigenetic alterations · Dysbiosis · Chronic inflammation

:::evidence tier="A"
Time-restricted eating improves insulin sensitivity and inflammatory markers in human trials. Wastyk et al. 2021 (PMID 34432955): fermented foods increase microbiome diversity and decrease inflammatory proteins over 10 weeks.
:::

## 2. The protocol — what to actually do

| Behavior | Target | Frequency | Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Eating window | 8–10 hours (16:8+ fast) | Daily | Tier A |
| Fiber | 30–40 g/day | Daily | Tier A |
| Plant diversity | 30+ species/week | Weekly | Tier A |
| Protein | 1.2–1.6 g/kg/day | Daily | Tier A |
| Fermented foods | 2–6 servings/day | Daily | Tier A |
| Refined sugar | Minimize | Daily | Tier A |

### Daily schedule template

| Time | Action | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Wake + 1h | Break fast (protein-forward) | Largest meals earlier |
| Midday | Fiber + polyphenol load | Vegetables, legumes, colorful plants |
| 3h pre-bed | Last meal | Supports [Sleep](/library/lifestyle/sleep) + autophagy |
| Weekly | Add 1 new plant species | Diversity counter |

:::practical title="Week-one implementation"
**Day 1–3:** Log current eating window and fiber grams only — no other changes.

**Day 4–7:** Tighten window to 10 hours. Add one high-fiber food (berries, lentils, chia).

**Week 2+:** Target 30 g fiber. Start fermented food 1–2 servings/day. Audit methyl donor checklist.

**Environment hacks:** Meal prep Sundays, visible fruit bowl, pre-portioned nuts, fermented food in fridge at eye level.
:::

:::decision title="Which protocol variant fits you?"
node | Eating window already ≤10 hours and fiber ≥25 g/day?
yes | node | hs-CRP still >1.0?
  yes | Ramp fermented foods to 4–6/day per [Dysbiosis](/library/hallmarks/dysbiosis) protocol
  no | Maintain; consider [NRF2 Triad](/library/synergies/glynac-nrf2-triad) only if lifestyle locked 8+ weeks
no | node | Type 2 diabetes or metformin therapy?
  yes | Physician-guided TRE — hypoglycemia risk during fasted windows
  no | 16:8 TRE sprint 4 weeks before any supplement stack
redflag | Eating disorder history, BMI <18.5, or blood in stool — no unsupervised caloric restriction
:::

## 5. Red flags

:::redflag title="When lifestyle isn't enough — or isn't safe"
- **Blood in stool, unintentional weight loss, or severe GI pain** — gastroenterology workup before fermented food ramps
- **Type 1 diabetes or insulin therapy** — fasting requires physician protocol
- **Eating disorder history** — TRE can trigger relapse; dietitian oversight
:::

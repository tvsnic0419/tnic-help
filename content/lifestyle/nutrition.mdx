## 1. Overview

Nutrition modulates **nutrient sensing** (mTOR/AMPK), supplies methyl donors for epigenetic maintenance, and provides building blocks for endogenous antioxidant synthesis (glycine, cysteine).

:::evidence tier="A"
Time-restricted eating, adequate protein, and polyphenol-rich diets show measurable effects on epigenetic clocks, insulin sensitivity, and inflammatory markers in human trials.
:::

:::visual
**Suggested visual:** Plate diagram — 30% protein, 40% vegetables/polyphenols, 20% complex carbs, 10% healthy fats. Annotations: glycine sources, broccoli sprouts (sulforaphane food synergy), methyl donors.
:::

## 4. Methyl donor checklist

- [ ] B12 (methylcobalamin or adequate dietary)
- [ ] Folate (leafy greens or methylfolate)
- [ ] Choline (eggs, liver, or supplement)
- [ ] Betaine (beets, wheat germ)
- [ ] Adequate protein (glycine — also from collagen/bone broth)

**Why it matters for NMN users:** NAD+ metabolism increases nicotinamide methylation — methyl donor adequacy prevents drain.

## 1. Synergy rationale

Antioxidant defense requires three layers — not one compound:

| Layer | Compound | Role | Analogy |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Substrate | GlyNAC | Provides glycine + cysteine for GSH synthesis | Fuel |
| Signal | Sulforaphane | NRF2 → 200+ protective genes including GCLC/GCLM | Amplifier |
| Recycle | R-ALA | Regenerates GSH from GSSG; mitochondrial redox | Recycler |

Using only one layer leaves gaps: NAC without NRF2 induction under-expresses synthesis enzymes; NRF2 without substrate starves the pathway; either without R-ALA accelerates GSSG accumulation.

**Suggested visual:** Three-layer shield diagram — inner (GSH pool), middle (NRF2 gene battery), outer (R-ALA redox cycle). Arrows showing ROS interception at each layer.
:::

## 2. Mechanistic synergy map

```
ROS attack
    ↓
[GlyNAC] → GSH direct neutralization
    ↓ (if overwhelmed)
[Sulforaphane] → NRF2 → ↑ GCLC, GCLM, NQO1, HO-1, GST
    ↓
[R-ALA] → GSSG → GSH regeneration + vit C/E recycling
    ↓
Reduced oxidative damage → Genomic stability + Mito protection + ↓ Inflammation
```

## 6. Contraindications & cautions

- **Chemotherapy:** Antioxidant stacks may interfere — physician clearance required
- **NAC + nitroglycerin:** Potential interaction
- **High-dose R-ALA:** Hypoglycemia risk in diabetics on medication — monitor glucose
- **Thyroid:** NAC may affect T4 conversion at high doses — monitor if hypothyroid

## 1. Synergy rationale

SIRT1 is **NAD+-dependent**. Resveratrol increases SIRT1 activity but cannot overcome insufficient NAD+ pools. NMN restores NAD+ but does not directly activate SIRT1. Together: **cofactor + activator**.

:::visual
**Suggested visual:** Lock-and-key diagram — NAD+ (key from NMN) + resveratrol (activator) → SIRT1 → deacetylation targets (PGC-1α, p53, NF-κB). Split AM/PM timeline below.
:::

## 2. Mechanism

| Step | Compound | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| AM | NMN | ↑ NAD+ pools |
| PM | Resveratrol | SIRT1 allosteric activation |
| Output | Both | PGC-1α → mitochondrial biogenesis; epigenetic stability; ↓ NF-κB |

**Why split timing:**
- NMN absorption optimized AM fasted
- Resveratrol benefits from fat co-ingestion (PM dinner)
- Mimics natural circadian NAD+ rhythm (peaks daytime)

## 3. Evidence

| Study | Finding | Tier |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Igarashi 2022 (36482258) | NMN ↑ NAD+ in humans | A |
| Baur 2006 (17028500) | Resveratrol improves health in high-calorie fed mice | Preclinical |
| PMID 30930169 | NAD+ precursors + sirtuin activators synergize for mito health | Review/mechanistic |

:::evidence tier="B"
Individual legs strong; combination human RCT limited. Mechanistic synergy well-established; graded Tier B for the pair.
:::

## 4. Dosing schedule

| Time | Compound | Dose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| AM (fasted) | NMN | 250–500 mg |
| PM (with fat) | Trans-resveratrol | 150–500 mg |

**Optional addition:** Ca-AKG 1 g AM for epigenetic TET support (see NAD+ Mito Stack).

## 5. When to skip resveratrol

- On blood thinners (theoretical CYP interaction — consult physician)
- Poor PM fat intake (absorption drops)
- GI sensitivity — try lower dose 150 mg
- If NMN alone achieves target NAD+ and subjective goals at 12 wk

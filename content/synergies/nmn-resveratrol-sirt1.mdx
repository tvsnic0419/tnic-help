> **TL;DR** — SIRT1 needs NAD+ (NMN) and an activator (resveratrol) — neither alone is complete. AM fasted NMN + PM fat-paired resveratrol mimics circadian NAD+ rhythm. Run 12–24 weeks; NAD+ index at week 4 is the primary checkpoint.

## 1. Why this stack exists

**SIRT1** deacetylates histones, PGC-1α, and metabolic regulators — but it is strictly **NAD+-dependent**. Resveratrol allosterically boosts SIRT1 activity yet cannot overcome empty NAD+ pools. NMN restores NAD+ but does not directly activate SIRT1.

| Layer | Compound | Role | Analogy |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Cofactor** | NMN | Restores NAD+ pools | Key |
| **Activator** | Resveratrol | Allosteric SIRT1 boost | Turn |

**Gap analysis — what breaks when you use only one:**

- NMN alone → NAD+ rises; SIRT1/PGC-1α signaling may stay flat without activator
- Resveratrol alone → SIRT1 stimulated but starved of cosubstrate — "revving without fuel"
- Combined → cofactor + activator completes the deacetylation cycle

```
AM (fasted):  [NMN] → NAD+ pool ↑ → SIRT3 mitophagy + PARP substrate
                    ↓

                    ↓
Output: PGC-1α → mitochondrial biogenesis
        Histone deacetylation → epigenetic stability
        NF-κB modulation → ↓ inflammaging
```

**Hallmarks addressed:** Mitochondrial dysfunction · Epigenetic alterations · Disabled autophagy · Disabled macroautophagy · Chronic inflammation — with mechanistic complementarity (not redundancy).

## 4. AM/PM choreography (non-negotiable spacing)

| Step | Time | Compound | Dose | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | AM fasted (wake + 30 min) | NMN | 250 mg → 500 mg | Titrate over 4 weeks |
| 2 | PM dinner (with fat) | Trans-resveratrol | 150 mg → 250–500 mg | ≥15 g dietary fat |
| 3 | Optional week 8+ | Ca-AKG | 1 g AM | Upgrade to [NAD+ Mito Stack](/library/synergies/nad-mito-stack) |

**Why spacing matters:** NMN absorption is optimized fasted AM; resveratrol needs fat co-ingestion PM. Split timing mimics natural circadian NAD+ rhythm (daytime peak) and avoids competing absorption windows.

:::practical title="Day-one setup (15 minutes)"
1. Label AM and PM bottles physically separate
2. Set wake alarm + dinner alarm for dose pairing
3. Baseline NAD+ index (or schedule week-4 draw)
4. Log AM energy and sleep quality daily in journal
5. Read [NMN](/library/compounds/nmn) and [Resveratrol](/library/compounds/resveratrol) deep dives
:::

## 6. Red flags and contraindications

:::redflag title="Hard stops — do not run this stack without clearance"
- **Warfarin, antiplatelet agents, or bleeding disorder** — resveratrol theoretical platelet effects
- **Insulin or sulfonylurea therapy** — AMPK activation risks hypoglycemia
- **Pregnancy or breastfeeding** — insufficient safety data for high-dose NAD+ precursors
:::

:::warning title="Soft cautions"
Resveratrol GI upset — take with largest fat-containing meal. NMN afternoon dosing may disrupt sleep in sensitive individuals — keep AM only. Do not combine high-dose NMN + NR without physician oversight.
:::

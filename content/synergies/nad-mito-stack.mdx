> **TL;DR** — Three non-redundant legs: NMN (NAD+ fuel), Ca-AKG (TCA + epigenetic support), resveratrol PM (SIRT1 → PGC-1α biogenesis). Highest mitochondrial hallmark coverage in the TNiC OTC catalog. Run 24 weeks; track NAD+ at week 4 and resting HR weekly.

## 1. Why this stack exists

Mitochondrial aging is not one failure — it is **energy depletion, poor recycling, and weak biogenesis** happening together. Single-compound protocols often fix one leg while leaving the wheel wobbling.

| Layer | Compound | Role | Analogy |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Fuel** | NMN | Restores NAD+ for SIRT3 mitophagy + PARP | Battery |
| **Flux** | Ca-AKG | TCA cycle anaplerosis + TET cofactor | Engine timing |
| **Signal** | Resveratrol | SIRT1 → PGC-1α biogenesis | Ignition |

**Gap analysis — what breaks when you use only one:**

- NMN alone → NAD+ rises but biogenesis signaling may stay flat without SIRT1/PGC-1α drive
- Resveratrol alone → SIRT1 activation starved without NAD+ cosubstrate
- Ca-AKG alone → metabolic support without NAD+-dependent quality control

## 4. AM/PM choreography (ramp-in protocol)

| Step | Week | Time | Compound | Dose | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | 1–2 | AM fasted | NMN | 250 mg | Establish NAD+ response |
| 2 | 3+ | AM breakfast | Ca-AKG | 1 g → 2 g | Titrate GI tolerance |
| 3 | 4+ | PM dinner | Resveratrol | 150–250 mg → 500 mg | With dietary fat |

**Why ramp-in matters:** GI tolerance and glucose shifts are easier to attribute when legs are staggered. Full triad on day one obscures which compound caused nausea or hypoglycemia.

:::practical title="Day-one setup (15 minutes)"
1. **Week 1 pill organizer:** NMN AM slot only — confirm NAD+ response before adding legs
2. **Log resting HR + morning energy** daily in Labs hub or notes
3. **Baseline labs:** NAD+ index minimum; add fasting glucose if metabolic risk
4. **Set week 4 review** — add resveratrol only if NAD+ moved or energy ↑1 point
5. **Cross-read** [NMN compound guide](/library/compounds/nmn) for methyl donor notes
:::

## 6. Red flags and contraindications

:::redflag title="Hard stops — do not run this stack without clearance"
- **Active cancer treatment** — NAD+ and polyphenol stacks require oncologist approval
- **Pregnancy or breastfeeding** — insufficient safety data across legs
- **Diabetes on insulin/sulfonylureas** — resveratrol and Ca-AKG may lower glucose; monitor weekly × 4 weeks
- **Anticoagulants** — resveratrol platelet effects; INR monitoring if on warfarin
- **Rising resting HR** despite improved fitness — arrhythmia workup before escalating NAD+
:::

:::warning title="Soft cautions"
Ca-AKG can cause loose stool at 2 g — split AM/PM doses. Resveratrol is light-sensitive; store capped. NMN quality varies by brand — if NAD+ flat at week 4 with perfect compliance, verify third-party testing.
:::

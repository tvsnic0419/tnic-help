> **TL;DR** — NR is a legitimate NAD+ precursor with human RCT data. TNiC standardizes on NMN for stack integration, but NR is defensible if your physician prefers it or you already respond. Never combine high-dose NR + NMN without oversight.

## 1. NR vs NMN — same destination, different molecule

Both nicotinamide riboside (NR) and NMN feed the NAD+ salvage pathway:

```
NR → NMN → NAD+ → SIRT1/3, PARP, CD38 balance
```

NR requires one enzymatic step (NRK) before becoming NMN. In practice, **both raise NAD+ metabolites in humans** — the debate is dose economics, stack integration, and individual response — not whether NAD+ restoration works.

:::evidence tier="A"
Martens 2018 (PMID 29514064): NR elevated NAD+ in healthy adults. Trammell 2016 and subsequent RCTs confirm human PK. Compare head-to-head in [NMN vs NR](/library/compare/nmn-vs-nr).
:::

## 3. Evidence summary

| Study | Molecule | Dose | Outcome | Tier |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Martens 2018 | NR | 1000 mg | ↑ whole-blood NAD+ | A |
| Trammell 2016 | NR | 100–1000 mg | ↑ NAD+ metabolites | A |
| Igarashi 2022 | NMN | 250 mg | ↑ NAD+, functional signal | A |

**Honest framing:** Tier A for NAD+ elevation. No human longevity endpoint trial for either precursor. Mouse data supports both.

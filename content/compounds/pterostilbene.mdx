> **TL;DR** — Pterostilbene is a methylated resveratrol analog with roughly 4× human oral bioavailability. Tier B: Kapetanovic 2011 confirmed safety and superior PK; longevity outcomes rely on SIRT1/AMPK mechanism shared with resveratrol. 50–150 mg PM with NMN AM; compare at /library/compare/resveratrol-vs-pterostilbene.

## 1. What pterostilbene does (and why it matters)

Trans-resveratrol's limitation is rapid glucuronidation. Pterostilbene's dimethyl structure slows clearance, achieving higher plasma exposure at lower doses — the same SIRT1 activation thesis with better pharmacokinetics.

**Primary hallmarks targeted:** Mitochondrial dysfunction · Epigenetic alterations · Chronic inflammation

:::evidence tier="B"
PMID 21749330 — Pharmacokinetics and safety of pterostilbene in humans (Kapetanovic 2011). See also TNiC compare table: /library/compare/resveratrol-vs-pterostilbene.
:::

## 2. Mechanism

| Pathway | Mechanism | Hallmark link |
| --- | --- | --- |
| SIRT1 activation | Allosteric sirtuin stimulation (NAD+-dependent) | Epigenetic · Mito |
| AMPK phosphorylation | Nutrient-sensing shift toward repair | Autophagy |
| NF-κB modulation | Anti-inflammatory transcription | Inflammation |

:::practical title="When pterostilbene beats resveratrol"
Choose pterostilbene when:

- You want SIRT1 signaling with fewer capsules / higher PK
- PM dosing with AM NMN is already in your stack
- GI tolerance to high-dose resveratrol is poor

## 3. Evidence summary

| Study | Design | Key outcomes | Tier |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Kapetanovic 2011 | Human PK/safety | 350 mg/day tolerated; higher exposure vs resveratrol | B |
| Preclinical | Animal | SIRT1/metabolic markers | B/C |

**Consensus:** Bioavailability advantage is human-confirmed; longevity outcomes remain mechanistic extrapolation — same Tier B posture as resveratrol.

## 4. Dosing protocol

- **Dose:** 50–150 mg pterostilbene
- **Timing:** PM with fat-containing meal (pairs with AM NMN)
- **Avoid:** Duplicating both resveratrol and pterostilbene without reason
- **Retest:** NAD+ proxy markers at week 4 if on NMN co-stack

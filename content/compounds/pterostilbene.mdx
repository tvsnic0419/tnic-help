> **TL;DR** — Pterostilbene is a methylated resveratrol analog with **4× greater human oral bioavailability** due to its dimethyl ether groups slowing glucuronidation. It activates SIRT1, AMPK, and Nrf2 via the same caloric-restriction-mimetic pathways as resveratrol — with more drug-like pharmacokinetics at a lower dose. Human data (Kapetanovic 2011, Murphy 2014) confirms safety and PK; SIRT1/metabolic outcomes are mechanistic extrapolation from the resveratrol evidence base. Dose: 50–150 mg PM with fat-containing meal. Tier B.

## 1. What pterostilbene does (and why it matters)

Trans-resveratrol's Achilles heel is rapid first-pass glucuronidation in the gut wall and liver — oral bioavailability is only 0.5–1% for free resveratrol. The vast majority of a resveratrol dose is metabolized before reaching systemic circulation.

Pterostilbene is resveratrol's methylated cousin, found naturally in blueberries and Indian kino tree (Pterocarpus marsupium). The key structural difference: two methoxy groups replace two hydroxyl groups on the stilbene scaffold. This small change has outsized pharmacokinetic consequences:

- **Dimethyl structure** resists glucuronidation → slower hepatic clearance
- **Plasma half-life** approximately 105 minutes vs ~14 minutes for resveratrol
- **Oral bioavailability** ~80% in preclinical models; human PK studies confirm roughly 4× higher plasma exposure at equivalent doses
- **CNS penetration** improved versus resveratrol (relevant for cognitive/BDNF effects)

This means pterostilbene provides SIRT1 activation, AMPK induction, and NF-κB suppression at one-quarter the dose of resveratrol — with more predictable tissue-level exposure.

**Primary hallmarks targeted:** Epigenetic Alterations (SIRT1) · Mitochondrial Dysfunction (AMPK/mitophagy) · Chronic Inflammation (NF-κB) · Cellular Senescence (secondary)

:::evidence tier="B"
Kapetanovic 2011 (Cancer Prevention Research, PMID 21749330): Human PK/safety trial, n=80 adults, dose escalation 50–250 mg/day × 2 weeks. Primary outcomes: plasma pterostilbene (and metabolites), safety profile. Result: 250 mg/day tolerated without serious AEs; plasma levels dose-proportional; confirmed superior PK vs historical resveratrol data. First human evidence at multi-dose levels.
:::

## 2. Mechanism — why methylation changes everything

### 2A. SIRT1 activation (primary longevity target)

Like resveratrol, pterostilbene allosterically activates SIRT1 — the histone deacetylase that drives epigenetic maintenance, DNA repair via FOXO3a, mitochondrial biogenesis via PGC-1α, and senescent cell SASP suppression. The difference: pterostilbene achieves this at 50–100 mg vs 500 mg+ for resveratrol's equivalent plasma levels.

SIRT1 activity requires NAD+ as a cofactor — this is why pterostilbene (PM) pairs with NMN or NR (AM) in TNiC stack design. The combination delivers both the fuel (NAD+) and the activating signal (pterostilbene) for SIRT1, separated in time to optimize peak effects.

### 2B. AMPK activation

Pterostilbene phosphorylates AMPK (AMP-activated protein kinase) — the cellular energy sensor that shifts the cell from growth mode to repair/longevity mode. AMPK activation:
- Inhibits mTORC1 (pro-aging growth pathway)
- Activates ULK1 → autophagy initiation
- Promotes mitochondrial biogenesis via PGC-1α
- Reduces fatty acid synthesis while promoting β-oxidation

### 2C. NF-κB suppression and anti-inflammaging

Pterostilbene inhibits IκB kinase (IKK), blocking NF-κB nuclear translocation. At 100 mg/day in the Murphy 2014 human trial, this translated to measurable reductions in inflammatory markers and blood pressure. The anti-inflammatory mechanism is especially relevant in the context of inflammaging — the chronic low-grade inflammatory state that accelerates after age 40.

:::practical title="When pterostilbene earns its stack slot"
Pterostilbene earns a place when **two or more** apply:

- Running AM NMN or NR and want PM SIRT1 activation to complete the sirtuin timing stack
- GI tolerance to high-dose resveratrol is poor (pterostilbene achieves equivalent SIRT1 signaling at ≤150 mg)
- ApoB or LDL trending elevated (100 mg/day reduced LDL in Murphy 2014)
- Systolic BP 120–139 range (Murphy 2014: −7.2 mmHg SBP at 100 mg/day in healthy adults)
- Cognitive or neuroprotection priority (superior CNS penetration vs resveratrol)
- Choosing one SIRT1 activator: pterostilbene is pharmacokinetically more reliable

**Skip or defer** if on anticoagulants without clearance; if stacking with full-dose resveratrol (diminishing returns above 100 mg PTB + 250 mg RSV). Do not combine at maximum doses of both without reason.
:::

| Bhatt 2012 | Animal metabolic study | Rats | Various | 6 weeks | AMPK activation, adipogenesis suppression, glucose tolerance improvement | C (animal) |
| Zhang 2013 (PLoS ONE) | In vitro + mouse | — | — | — | SIRT1 activation dose-response; CNS penetration confirmed in brain tissue | C |
| Rimando 2002 (J Agri Food Chem) | Analytical chemistry | — | — | — | Natural source quantification; blueberry 99 nmol/g; first identification of pterostilbene as AMPK activator | — |

**Consensus:** The pharmacokinetic advantage over resveratrol is human-confirmed. The mechanistic case for SIRT1/AMPK/NF-κB is robust across cell lines and animal models. Human efficacy endpoints (longevity, metabolic disease outcomes) are limited to the Murphy 2014 blood pressure and LDL data. This is characteristic Tier B — solid mechanistic + PK foundation, limited human outcome endpoints.

**Important nuance from Murphy 2014:** At 250 mg/day, LDL *increased* in some subjects. At 100 mg/day, LDL decreased. This dose-dependent inversion is not fully explained but may relate to SIRT1 effects on cholesterol metabolism. **Stay at ≤150 mg/day unless monitoring ApoB/LDL closely.**

## 6. Safety, red flags, and contraindications

- **Confirmed human safety at 250 mg/day** — Kapetanovic 2011, n=80, 2 weeks, no serious AEs
- **Mild antiplatelet properties** — like resveratrol; clinically relevant only at high doses or with anticoagulant co-use
- **LDL dose caveat** — Murphy 2014 LDL increase at 250 mg/day is the primary dosing guardrail; stay ≤150 mg without monitoring

:::redflag title="Pterostilbene safety notes"
- **Anticoagulants (warfarin, apixaban, rivaroxaban)** — antiplatelet properties require physician clearance; inform prescriber before starting
- **High-dose caveat (≥250 mg/day)** — LDL elevation risk in some subjects per Murphy 2014; do not exceed 150 mg without ApoB monitoring
- **Pre-surgery** — stop 2 weeks before any procedure due to antiplatelet activity
- **Resveratrol duplication** — stacking high-dose resveratrol (>250 mg) with pterostilbene (>100 mg) provides diminishing SIRT1 return with additive antiplatelet risk; choose one or keep proportional
- **Pregnancy/lactation** — insufficient human safety data at supplemental doses; defer
:::

## 1. Overview

Sulforaphane is an isothiocyanate from cruciferous vegetables that **activates NRF2** — the master regulator of phase-II detox, antioxidant, and proteasome genes. A single NRF2 activation event can upregulate 200+ cytoprotective genes.

**Hallmarks:** Genomic instability · Chronic inflammation · Loss of proteostasis

:::evidence tier="A"
PMID 18454171: sulforaphane modifies KEAP1 cysteine residues, stabilizing NRF2 for nuclear translocation. Human airway epithelial cell studies confirm dose-dependent NRF2 target gene induction.
:::

:::visual
**Suggested visual:** KEAP1-NRF2 release mechanism — sulforaphane covalent binding → NRF2 nuclear translocation → ARE gene battery (NQO1, HO-1, GCLC, GST).
:::

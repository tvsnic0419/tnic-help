## 1. Overview

TUDCA is a **hydrophilic bile acid** that acts as a chemical chaperone, stabilizing protein folding and reducing endoplasmic reticulum (ER) stress. It supports proteostasis, mitochondrial membrane integrity, and autophagy — hallmarks that compound with age.

**Primary hallmarks targeted:** Loss of proteostasis · Mitochondrial dysfunction · Disabled macroautophagy

:::evidence tier="B"
Strong preclinical data for ER stress reduction, apoptosis inhibition, and mitochondrial protection. Human data strongest in cholestatic liver disease; longevity-specific human RCTs do not exist.
:::

## 2. Mechanism

### 2.1 ER stress & UPR modulation

Aging cells accumulate misfolded proteins. The unfolded protein response (UPR) attempts correction but chronic activation triggers apoptosis. TUDCA:

- Stabilizes protein folding in the ER lumen
- Reduces CHOP-mediated apoptotic signaling
- Lowers inflammatory outputs from stressed cells

### 2.2 Mitochondrial protection

TUDCA prevents Bax translocation to mitochondria, reducing cytochrome c release. This directly supports the mitochondrial hallmark independent of bile acid physiology.

### 2.3 Autophagy support

By reducing ER stress burden, cellular resources shift toward productive autophagic clearance rather than damage control.

| Pathway | TUDCA action | Hallmark |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Protein folding | Chemical chaperone | Proteostasis |
| UPR | ATF6/IRE1 modulation | Proteostasis |
| Mitochondria | Anti-apoptotic membrane stabilization | Mitochondrial dysfunction |
| Autophagy | Reduced ER burden → improved flux | Disabled autophagy |

## 6. Safety & contraindications

- **GI effects:** Loose stools at higher doses — reduce dose or take with food
- **Gallstones:** UDCA/TUDCA class can affect bile composition — consult if known gallbladder disease
- **Pregnancy:** Avoid
- **Not in TNiC stack toggles:** Library-only reference; evaluate with physician if on other bile acid therapies

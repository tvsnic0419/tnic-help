> **TL;DR** — EPA and DHA are the only omega-3 fatty acids with meaningful human trial data. REDUCE-IT (NEJM 2018, n=8,179) showed 25% reduction in major CV events with 4 g/day EPA. The mechanism is not just anti-inflammatory suppression — EPA/DHA generate specialized pro-resolving mediators (SPMs) that actively resolve inflammation. Dose 2–4 g combined EPA+DHA daily with a fat-containing meal. Retest at 3 months: omega-3 index, hs-CRP, triglycerides.

## 1. What omega-3 does (and why it matters)

The omega-3 story is complicated by decades of oversimplified messaging ("fish oil is anti-inflammatory"). The real mechanism is more precise: EPA and DHA are not merely inflammation suppressors — they are precursors to an entirely distinct class of bioactive lipid mediators called specialized pro-resolving mediators (SPMs), which include resolvins, protectins, and maresins.

SPMs actively terminate inflammation and restore tissue homeostasis — a fundamentally different mechanism from NSAIDs or corticosteroids that simply block inflammatory signals at their origin. The chronic low-grade inflammation of aging ("inflammaging") is characterized not just by elevated pro-inflammatory signals but by impaired resolution. EPA/DHA address both.

**Primary hallmarks targeted:** Chronic Inflammation · Intercellular Communication · Telomere Attrition · Dysbiosis

:::evidence tier="A"
Bhatt 2019 REDUCE-IT (NEJM, PMID 30145934): 8,179 patients on statins with elevated TG; icosapentaenoic acid (EPA, 4 g/day) vs mineral oil placebo; 4.9-year follow-up. Primary composite: 25% RRR in major adverse CV events (MI, stroke, CV death, revascularization, unstable angina). Landmark cardiovascular trial with the highest omega-3 dose studied.
:::

## 2. Mechanism — active resolution, not passive suppression

| Pathway | Mechanism | Hallmark link |
| --- | --- | --- |
| SPM synthesis | EPA → E-series resolvins; DHA → D-series resolvins + protectins | Inflammation resolution |
| NF-κB suppression | EPA/DHA compete with arachidonic acid for COX/LOX → fewer pro-inflammatory eicosanoids | Chronic inflammation |
| Telomere maintenance | Omega-3 index inversely associated with telomere attrition rate | Telomere attrition |
| Microbiome diversity | EPA/DHA reshape gut flora, increase SCFA producers, reduce dysbiosis markers | Dysbiosis |
| SASP modulation | Resolvin E1 reduces IL-6, IL-8 — key SASP components | Cellular senescence |
| Triglyceride reduction | VLDL synthesis inhibition → TG −20–35% at 4 g/day | Cardiovascular aging |

:::practical title="When omega-3 is worth your stack slot"
Omega-3 earns a place when **one or more** apply:

- Elevated hs-CRP (>1.0 mg/L) without clear infectious cause — inflammaging signal
- Elevated triglycerides (>150 mg/dL) — omega-3 is the most evidence-graded TG reduction tool
- Omega-3 index <8% (standard Western diet is typically 4–5%)
- Building any longevity stack — omega-3 is the broadest-coverage Tier A compound in the library

**Skip or defer** if you regularly eat 3+ servings of fatty fish per week (mackerel, sardines, salmon) and omega-3 index is verified ≥8%.
:::

## 3. Evidence summary

| Study | Design | N | Duration | Key outcomes | Tier |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| REDUCE-IT 2018 (PMID 30145934) | RCT, statin + elevated TG | 8,179 | 4.9 years | MACE −25%, CV death −20% | A |
| Farzaneh-Far 2010 (PMID 22245710) | RCT telomere/omega-3 | 608 | 5 years | Higher omega-3 index → slower telomere attrition | A |
| Costantini 2021 (PMID 32835509) | Meta-analysis, microbiome | — | — | Omega-3 significantly increased microbiome diversity, SCFA producers | A |
| AREDS2 2013 | RCT, AMD | 4,203 | 5 years | No benefit in AMD (different population; TG/inflammation endpoints not primary) | A |

**Consensus:** Tier A for cardiovascular outcomes (REDUCE-IT), Tier A for telomere maintenance association, Tier A for microbiome diversity. The AREDS2 null result for AMD does not diminish the cardiovascular and metabolic evidence — different endpoint, different population. Quality and dose matter: pharmaceutical-grade EPA (Vascepa/icosapentaenoic acid) was used in REDUCE-IT; standard fish oil includes both EPA and DHA.

:::decision title="Should you start omega-3?"
node | Do you have elevated TG (>150), hs-CRP (>1.0), or omega-3 index <8%?

no | Regular high-quality fatty fish consumption? Measure omega-3 index to confirm before adding supplement.
redflag | On blood thinners → high-dose EPA/DHA has mild anticoagulant effect → physician review at >3 g/day
:::

## 4. Dosing protocol

| Parameter | Recommendation | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Dose | 2–4 g combined EPA+DHA | REDUCE-IT used 4 g EPA (Vascepa); 2 g is a reasonable maintenance dose for lower-risk |
| Form | rTG (re-esterified triglyceride) or ethyl ester | rTG has ~70% better absorption vs ethyl ester; krill oil has enhanced phospholipid absorption |
| Timing | With fat-containing meal | Critical — lipid-soluble, requires dietary fat for absorption |
| Duration | 12 weeks for TG/hs-CRP signal | Omega-3 index plateaus at 3–4 months |

:::practical title="Week-one compliance checklist"
- [ ] Check form: rTG or phospholipid (krill) preferred over standard ethyl ester
- [ ] Take with largest meal of the day
- [ ] Test baseline: omega-3 index, TG, hs-CRP, ApoB
- [ ] Set 3-month retest reminder for all four markers
:::

## 6. Safety, red flags, and contraindications

- **Excellent safety profile at standard doses** — extensive post-market data across 40+ years of clinical use
- **Mild anticoagulant effect at high doses** (>3 g/day) — clinically significant mainly when combined with blood thinners
- **Fish burp** — enteric-coated or rTG forms minimize GI side effects; freezing capsules also helps

:::redflag title="Do not self-start without clearance"
- **Warfarin / apixaban co-use** — additive antiplatelet effect at >3 g/day; physician review and INR monitoring
- **Pre-surgery** — stop 2 weeks before elective procedures
- **Atrial fibrillation** — REDUCE-IT showed a small increase in AF incidence; patients with AF history should discuss with cardiologist
:::

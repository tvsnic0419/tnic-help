> **TL;DR** — Taurine is a sulfur-containing amino acid that buffers mitochondrial and membrane stress; blood levels fall sharply with age. Tier B: Singh 2023 *Science* linked deficiency to aging hallmarks and extended lifespan in mice. 1–3 g/day; retest subjective energy and hs-CRP at week 12.

## 1. What taurine does (and why it matters)

Taurine concentrations in human blood decline roughly 80% between youth and old age. Singh et al. (PMID 37289866) showed taurine supplementation reversed multiple aging markers and extended lifespan across species — one of the strongest recent animal longevity signals for a simple OTC amino acid.

**Primary hallmarks targeted:** Mitochondrial dysfunction · Genomic instability · Chronic inflammation

:::evidence tier="B"
PMID 37289866 — Taurine deficiency as a driver of aging (*Science*, 2023). Strong animal lifespan and multi-hallmark data; human longevity endpoints not yet established in large RCTs.
:::

:::practical title="When taurine is worth your money"
Taurine earns a stack slot when **two or more** apply:

- Age 50+ with declining energy or exercise recovery
- Low dietary taurine (minimal seafood/meat)
- Complementary to NAD+ or glutathione restoration stacks

**Skip or defer** if you have kidney disease without physician clearance.
:::

## 3. Evidence summary

| Study | Design | Key outcomes | Tier |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Singh 2023 | Multi-species + human association | Lifespan extension mice; taurine correlates with healthspan | B |

**Consensus:** Mechanistically compelling and safe at food-supplement doses; treat as Tier B adjunct, not stack foundation until human longevity RCTs publish.

## 4. Dosing protocol

- **Start:** 1 g/day with food
- **Target:** 1–3 g/day split AM/PM
- **Duration:** 12+ weeks before judging
- **Retest:** Subjective energy log + hs-CRP if available

## 6. Safety, red flags, and contraindications

- **Generally well-tolerated** — taurine is a conditionally essential amino acid; adverse events rare at 1–4 g/day
- **Renal caution** — kidney disease reduces taurine clearance; physician clearance required before sustained use above 2 g/day

:::redflag title="Taurine safety notes"
- **Lithium users** — taurine may increase lithium excretion; monitor levels if co-prescribed
- **Energy drinks** — taurine in energy drinks is co-delivered with caffeine and B vitamins at extreme doses; supplement separately to isolate effects
- **GI sensitivity** — rare; split dose AM/PM to minimize
:::

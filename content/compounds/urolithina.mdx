> **TL;DR** — Urolithin A is the only commercially available compound proven in a Phase 2 RCT to activate mitophagy in humans. At 1000 mg/day (Mitopure standardized), it improved muscle strength and reduced fatigue in adults ≥65. Mitophagy — the selective clearance of damaged mitochondria — is one of the most powerful levers in mitochondrial aging. Allow 4–12 weeks; retest at muscle function benchmarks.

## 1. What urolithin A does (and why it matters)

Most people cannot absorb ellagitannins from pomegranate or walnuts efficiently enough to generate meaningful urolithin A levels — only ~40% of the population has the gut microbiome capable of converting ellagitannins to urolithin A. Mitopure is a standardized, directly bioavailable form that bypasses this conversion dependency.

Urolithin A activates mitophagy — the selective autophagy pathway that targets damaged mitochondria for lysosomal degradation. As mitochondria accumulate oxidative damage with age, mitophagy efficiency declines and damaged organelles persist, generating excess ROS and driving the mitochondrial dysfunction hallmark. Restoring mitophagy flux is mechanistically distinct from simply boosting NAD+ (NMN) or ETC cofactors (CoQ10) — it clears the damaged substrate entirely.

**Primary hallmarks targeted:** Mitochondrial Dysfunction · Disabled Autophagy · Cellular Senescence

:::evidence tier="A"
Ryu 2022 (Cell Reports Medicine, PMID 35391504): Phase 2 double-blind RCT, n=66 adults ≥65. 500 mg and 1000 mg Mitopure vs placebo, 4 months. Primary endpoint: muscle strength (handgrip, leg press). 1000 mg group showed significant improvement vs placebo in muscle endurance and fatigue reduction. Mitophagy gene expression confirmed via skeletal muscle biopsies.
:::

## 2. Mechanism — clearing damaged mitochondria, not just fueling them

NMN and CoQ10 support existing mitochondria. Urolithin A removes the damaged ones. This distinction matters:

| Pathway | Mechanism | Hallmark link |
| --- | --- | --- |
| PINK1/Parkin activation | Urolithin A induces mitochondrial membrane depolarization signal | Mitochondrial dysfunction |
| Mitophagy flux | Damaged mitochondria tagged and delivered to lysosome | Disabled autophagy |
| SASP reduction | Clearing dysfunctional organelles reduces ROS-driven inflammatory signaling | Cellular senescence |
| Muscle function | Improved mitochondrial quality in skeletal muscle → strength + endurance | Multi-hallmark |

:::practical title="When urolithin A is worth your stack slot"
Urolithin A earns a place when **two or more** apply:

- Age ≥45 with declining muscle recovery, endurance, or unexplained fatigue
- Currently on NMN or CoQ10 but want to add mitophagy clearance to the stack
- Gut microbiome issues suspected (low urolithin production from dietary sources)
- Building a senescence-focused protocol (Longevity Pro preset)

**Skip or defer** if you are below 35 with no mitochondrial stress signals — dietary sources (pomegranate, walnuts, raspberries) may suffice if gut flora supports conversion.
:::

## 3. Evidence summary

| Study | Design | N | Duration | Key outcomes | Tier |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Ryu 2022 (PMID 35391504) | Phase 2 RCT, n=66 adults ≥65 | 66 | 4 months | Muscle strength ↑, fatigue ↓, mitophagy genes confirmed in biopsy | A |
| Andreux 2019 (PMID 31230029) | Phase 1 RCT, 4-week safety | 60 | 4 weeks | Safe and well-tolerated at 250–2000 mg; mitochondrial gene expression dose-dependent | A |
| Rempel 2022 (PMID 27400265) | Preclinical + mechanistic | — | — | C. elegans lifespan extension; PINK1/Parkin pathway confirmed | B |

**Consensus:** Tier A for muscle function and mitophagy activation in older adults at 1000 mg standardized Mitopure. Phase 3 trials for sarcopenia and frailty endpoints underway. The core mechanistic claim — mitophagy activation in human skeletal muscle — is biopsy-confirmed.

## 6. Safety, red flags, and contraindications

- **Excellent safety profile** — Phase 1 showed no adverse events at 250–2000 mg; Phase 2 confirmed tolerability at 4 months
- **Loose stool reported** by some at 2000 mg — 1000 mg has minimal GI concern

:::redflag title="Do not self-start without clearance"
- **mTOR inhibitor co-use (rapamycin, everolimus)** — both target related autophagy pathways; interaction unstudied
- **Active cancer treatment** — mitophagy modulation may interact with chemotherapy mechanisms; physician review required
:::

## 1. Overview

NMN is a direct **NAD+ precursor** that bypasses the rate-limiting NAMPT enzyme step. Cellular NAD+ declines approximately **50% between ages 40–60**, starving sirtuins (SIRT1/3), PARP-mediated DNA repair, and mitophagy pathways.

**Primary hallmarks targeted:** Mitochondrial dysfunction · Genomic instability · Epigenetic alterations · Cellular senescence · Deregulated nutrient sensing

:::evidence tier="A"
Human trials demonstrate NMN elevates blood NAD+ metabolites within weeks. Igarashi 2022 (PMID 36482258): 250 mg/day increased NAD+ and improved physical performance markers in healthy adults.
:::

## 2. Mechanism

### 2.1 NAD+ → Sirtuin axis

| Enzyme | NAD+ dependency | Function | Hallmark |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| SIRT1 | High | Histone deacetylation, metabolic sensing | Epigenetic alterations |
| SIRT3 | High | Mitochondrial protein deacetylation, mitophagy | Mitochondrial dysfunction |
| PARP1 | Consumes NAD+ | DNA strand break repair | Genomic instability |
| CD38 | Degrades NAD+ | Inflammation-driven NAD+ sink | Inflammation |

### 2.2 NMN vs NR

Both are NAD+ precursors. NMN may require conversion to NR for cellular entry in some tissues (debated). Practically: both raise NAD+ metabolites in humans. TNiC standardizes on NMN based on trial dosing and stack integration.

### 2.3 Resveratrol synergy

Resveratrol **activates** SIRT1; NMN **fuels** it. Without adequate NAD+, sirtuin activation is enzymatically limited. See SIRT1 Activation Pair synergy guide.

## 6. Safety & considerations

- **Well tolerated** at 250–500 mg in published trials
- **Methyl donor drain:** High-dose NAD+ precursors may increase nicotinamide methylation — ensure adequate B12, folate, betaine if on long-term protocol
- **Flush risk:** Do not confuse with high-dose niacin (nicotinic acid)
- **Consult physician if:** Active cancer treatment, pregnancy, or on immunosuppressants

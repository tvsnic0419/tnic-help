## What happens

Every day, each cell endures **10,000–100,000 DNA lesions** from replication errors, oxidative stress, and environmental exposure. Young cells repair efficiently via base-excision repair (BER), nucleotide-excision repair (NER), and homologous recombination.

Aging erodes this capacity because:

1. Repair enzymes lose fidelity
2. NAD+ consumed by PARP during repair is not replenished fast enough
3. Oxidative damage outpaces antioxidant defenses (glutathione depletion)

## Why TNiC starts here

Genomic instability is the **first hallmark** in the López-Otín framework because DNA damage is the root substrate for cancer and accelerates every downstream aging process.

The TNiC defense stack addresses this through two parallel pathways:

- **NAD+ restoration (NMN)** — fuels PARP-mediated strand break repair
- **NRF2 activation (sulforaphane)** — upregulates NQO1, GST, and other protective enzymes

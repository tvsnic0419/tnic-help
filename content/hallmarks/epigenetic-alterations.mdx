> **TL;DR** — DNA methylation and histone marks drift with age; epigenetic clocks predict mortality better than birthdays alone. Ca-AKG (via TET enzymes) and NAD+-SIRT1 pathways are the highest-leverage TNiC levers; caloric moderation resets patterns in animal models. Track GrimAge or DunedinPACE every 6–12 months.

## What this hallmark means

**Epigenetics** controls which genes are expressed without changing the DNA sequence. With age, methylation patterns shift — CpG sites gain or lose methyl groups, histones acetylate differently, and chromatin compacts into "aged" configurations.

The **Horvath**, **GrimAge**, and **DunedinPACE** clocks quantify this drift. They predict cardiovascular events and all-cause mortality **independent of chronological age**. Yamanaka-factor experiments in mice prove epigenetic aging is **partially reversible** — the hallmark is not fixed destiny.

| Failure mode | What changes | What you feel |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Hypermethylation drift** | Tumor suppressor silencing | Subtle metabolic slowdown |
| **Hypomethylation chaos** | Retrotransposon activation | Inflammation + instability coupling |
| **Histone deacetylation loss** | Heterochromatin breaks down | Energy inconsistency, "biological age gap" |
| **TET enzyme decline** | Demethylation stalls without α-ketoglutarate | Epigenetic age runs ahead of calendar age |

## Why this matters for your protocol

Epigenetic age is where **multi-pathway stacks** earn their keep. TET dioxygenases need **α-ketoglutarate**; sirtuins need **NAD+**; both decline with age. Single-compound approaches often fail because they address only one enzymatic leg.

**Intervention hierarchy (evidence-weighted):**

1. **Ca-AKG** — TET cofactor; mouse lifespan extension data (PMID 33027664)
2. **NMN → SIRT1 epigenetic control** — NAD+-dependent histone deacetylation
3. **Resveratrol → SIRT1 activation** — phytoalexin allosteric boost (PMID 17909917)
4. **Caloric moderation / fasting** — resets methylation in animal models; pilot human clock reversal
5. **Epigenetic clock testing** — GrimAge, DunedinPACE, or TruAge to track response

:::evidence tier="A"
Ca-AKG extends lifespan and compresses morbidity in mice via TET-mediated epigenetic remodeling (PMID 33027664). NAD+-SIRT1 histone deacetylation is foundational to heterochromatin stability — human NMN trials confirm NAD+ restoration (PMID 36482258).
:::

## Red flags — when to pause or escalate

:::redflag title="Stop and consult before intensifying"
- **Active malignancy or recent chemotherapy** — epigenetic modulation requires oncology coordination
- **Pregnancy or breastfeeding** — insufficient safety data for AKG and high-dose NAD+ precursors
- **Unexplained weight loss + accelerated clock** — rule out occult disease before longevity stacking
:::

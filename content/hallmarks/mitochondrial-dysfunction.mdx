## What happens

Mitochondria are not static power plants — they are dynamic organelles that fuse, divide, and get recycled (mitophagy). With age:

- **NAD+ pools decline** ~50% between ages 40–60, starving sirtuins and PARP
- **Biogenesis slows** as PGC-1α signaling weakens
- **Damaged mitochondria accumulate** because mitophagy cannot keep pace

The result: less ATP, more reactive oxygen species (ROS), and a metabolic shift toward glycolysis even in well-oxygenated tissue.

## The TNiC lens

This is the hallmark with the **highest TNiC stack coverage (95%)** because our core compounds directly target the NAD+ → sirtuin → mitophagy axis.

> **Key insight:** Mitochondrial dysfunction is both a hallmark *and* an amplifier. Poor mito output increases ROS, which damages DNA (Hallmark 1), proteins (Hallmark 4), and triggers inflammation (Hallmark 10).
